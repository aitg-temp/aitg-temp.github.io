About Me

I completed my Bachelor's (H) chemistry from Delhi University, Delhi. After that, I completed M.Sc. Chemistry from NIT Nagpur, Maharashtra. I joined IISER Bhopal in 2015 and currently working as PhD student in ab initio theory group (AITG).

Research Interest

I am interested in developing of atomic-level understanding of catalytic reactions such as water splitting, methane activation, etc. on molecules and solid surfaces. I study the mechanisms and electronic structure of such reactions and identify the key factors which dictate the catalytic activity. The insightful understanding of interfaces between different materials may be a further useful strategy in order to control the key parameters and hence to design new efficient catalysts for specific catalytic reactions. Therefore, I also interested in modeling various kinds of interfaces to understand the realistic scenario in the context of a particular reaction. The overall the idea is aimed at designing cost-effective, earth-abundant and practically efficient catalysts to harness solar energy as well as environment-friendly hydrogen generation.
