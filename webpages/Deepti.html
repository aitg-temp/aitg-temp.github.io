About Me

Hello! I am Deepti Rajpoot. I have done my Master’s in Chemistry from Dr. Hari Singh Gour Central University Sagar, Madhya Pradesh. I have joined IISER Bhopal and ab initio theory group (AITG) in 2017 as CSIR-JRF fellow.

Research Interest

I am interested in exploring the structural, electronic and magnetic properties of bulk as well as epitaxially strained thin films transition metal double perovskite due to their fascinating properties in various areas like magnetism, ferroelectricity, spintronics, multiferroics, metal to insulator transition etc.

Transition metal double perovskites (DP) having general formula A2BB’O6 where A is alkali/alkaline earth metal cation, B and B’ are two different transition metal cation and O is oxygen. This is the general formula for B-site ordered double perovskite but it could be A-site ordered also with general formula AA’BB’O6/AA’B2O6. On changing the B-site/A-site cation, its electronic, structural and magnetic properties could be changed which leads to interesting phenomena such as ferroelectricity, magnetism, multiferroicity etc. which could be used in various applications as in memory storage devices, spintronics etc. Epitaxial strain on the DP and coupling of strain with other degree of freedoms also changes the electronic properties of the system which can give very different or same properties as bulk perovskite.

I am also working on solid materials which shows anomalous phenomena under temperature and pressure, for instances, Negative Linear Compressibility (NLC), Negative Thermal Compressibility (NTE) etc.
