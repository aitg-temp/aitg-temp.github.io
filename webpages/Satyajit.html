About Me

I completed my bachelor’s degree in Chemistry from Bankura Christian College in West Bengal and my master’s degree in Chemistry from NIT Durgapur, West Bengal. I joined ab initio theory group on 2015 as PhD student.

Research Interest

I am interested to understand photo-induced excited state phenomena in biological systems like DNA as well as small organic molecules. I have studied photo-induced self repair process in a specific oligonucleotide sequence using TDDFT method. Currently I am trying to understand prebiotically possible UV-induced processes which led to canonical nucleobases to present as the building block of DNA. I am also trying to understand key mechanism artificial and natural sunscreen molecules which can provide insightful understanding for designing better sunscreens.

Theoretical studies of such photo-induced processes can be done either statically by identifying specific nuclear coordinates which is responsible for particular photoreaction in multidimensional PES or dynamically by monitoring changes in electronic properties and nuclear coordinates at ultrafast time scale. Theses photo-induced phenomena includes nonadiabatic effects like internal conversion, intersystem crossing. It is very important to note that theoretical investigations of such photochemical processes will need a judicious choice of methods which can perform electron-ion dynamics at moderate computational cost. In our study we combine TDDFT with non-adiabatic surface hopping dynamics to investigate ultrafast excited state phenomena.
