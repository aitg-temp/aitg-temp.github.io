Research Interest

DNA absorbs in the UV region of solar spectrum and undergoes several photophysical and photochemical processes which lead to formation of harmful photoproducts like cyclobutane pyrimidine dimers (CPDs), 6-4 photo lesion etc. To protect the organism from harmful effects of UV light, nature has developed a defence mechanism so that electronically excited molecules quickly dissipate to ground electronic state via ultrafast internal conversion. During the chemical evolution, few enzymatic machineries were developed which can selectively repair of different types photodamage. A recent study demonstrates that an oligonucleotide has inherent capability to repair damage. This kind of photodamage and photorepair processes operate through electron transfer and/or energy transfer mechanism either via adiabatically or via non-adiabatically. Nonadiabatic effect like internal conversion, state crossing would add more complexity to this problem and hence complete understanding is still lacking.

We are studying the photo-induced processes related to the photostability of canonical nucleobases and self-repair mechanism of photolesion of DNA. We are using a combination of linear response (LR) and real time TDDFT to understand the mechanism of self repair. Based on real time propagation data we have developed a new methodology to understand electron transfer and energy transfer during whole time-propagation period. This method will give support to the time resolved experiment to understand complex photo-induced phenomena. We are also interested to study the inherent photo stability of canonical nucleobases and compare their photostability with non-canonical nucleobases which can answer about survival of canonical nucleobases at harsh conditions during prebiotic time. Here we combine TDDFT with nonadiabatic surface hopping dynamics to study deactivation mechanism of nucleobases. We are also working on natural sunscreen molecules to understand the key mechanism behind their UV protection capability which will help in designing better artificial sunscreen.
